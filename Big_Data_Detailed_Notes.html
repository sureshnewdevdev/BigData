Big Data Fundamentals

Big Data refers to datasets whose size, speed, and variety exceed the capability of traditional databases to capture, store, manage, and analyze.

The 5Vs

  • Volume – terabytes to petabytes of data.
  • Velocity – continuous streams (events, logs).
  • Variety – text, images, sensor data, nested JSON.
  • Veracity – data quality & trustworthiness.
  • Value – business outcomes (revenue, savings, risk).

Typical Uses

  • Customer 360 and personalization.
  • Fraud detection and anomaly alerts.
  • IoT telemetry analytics & predictive maintenance.
  • Clickstream/marketing attribution and A/B testing.

Architecture

Common Patterns

  • Data Lake + Warehouse (lake for raw, warehouse for curated marts).
  • Lakehouse (one platform supports ACID tables, BI & ML).
  • Lambda (batch + stream paths) and Kappa (stream‑first) architectures.

Reference Layers

LayerPurposeTypical Tech
RAW / BronzeImmutable landing of source dataGCS/S3/ADLS, Delta/Iceberg
SilverCleaned, typed, conformedSpark/DBT → Warehouse
GoldBusiness marts & KPIsBigQuery/Snowflake/Redshift

Benefits

  • 360° insights → better decisions and personalization.
  • Scalable storage & compute → handle peaks elastically.
  • Advanced analytics & ML → predictions, recommendations.
  • Cost efficiency with separation of storage/compute.

Challenges

  • Data quality, lineage, and metadata management.
  • Security & privacy (PII, access control, masking).
  • Cost governance (skewed joins, unpartitioned scans).
  • Skill gaps: distributed systems & SQL/ML expertise.
  • Ops: monitoring pipelines, retries, idempotency.
Mitigation: adopt naming/data contracts, unit tests for SQL, CI/CD for pipelines, and FinOps dashboards.

Data Lifecycle Stages

  1. Generate – events, transactions, sensors, files.
  2. Ingest – batch loads, streams, CDC connectors.
  3. Store – lake/lakehouse with versioned ACID tables.
  4. Process – batch & stream transforms; quality checks.
  5. Serve – marts, APIs, features for ML, dashboards.
  6. Archive/Dispose – retention policies, legal hold, deletion.

Cheat‑sheet & Checklist

  • Default to Parquet with partitioning; compact small files.
  • Choose ELT when warehouse is primary and SQL‑friendly; choose ETL for heavy pre‑warehouse cleansing.
  • Track lineage & data contracts; automate quality tests.
  • Secure by design: row/column‑level controls, tokenization, audit logs.
